In enterprise software, selling a product and selling a solution are not the same thing. A product is an artifact — it lets you automate some tasks, see analytics, comment on documents. A solution takes an existing process in a customer's organisation and transforms it into something that drives a meaningfully better outcome. The distinction matters because customers often struggle to close that gap themselves.

Qualtrics illustrates this clearly. The components — reporting, dashboards, analytics, surveys, sentiment analysis — are identical across different use cases. But a product manager trying to solve their specific problem with those components still needs help figuring out what to use and how. The Lego pieces alone aren't enough.

Apollo's root cause analysis program made this structural. They found that 80% of friction comes from 20% of issues, and those issues span the whole company: pricing and packaging, how credits work, onboarding, what the product promises vs what it delivers. None of those problems could be solved by product alone.

**The insight that came out of that work is direct: you cannot solve those customer problems unless the company is thinking about itself as the product and is unified to go solve it.** Marketing, sales, customer success, and product all need to be aligned on the same core users, use cases, and pain points.

With AI tools requiring significant process change on the customer side — as organisations shrink, roles shift, and outcomes become harder to define — this alignment becomes harder and more important at the same time. For the foreseeable future, the company is the product.
