This note combines two platform-focused drafts: one on platform-as-a-product and long-tail enablement, and one on internal AEC platform composability (VIKTOR, Azure PostgreSQL, and Grasshopper workflows). Together they frame platform strategy as both a product architecture decision and an operating model for scaling reusable engineering capability.

Referenced sources include Team Topologies on platform-as-a-product [R1], and platform engineering primer resources [R2][R5][R6], used to anchor and extend the note context.

## Key points

- Platform strategy should combine product thinking with reusable service architecture
- The long-tail model is relevant in AEC when many niche tools collectively create high value
- Product as a service can improve adoption when users consume outcomes rather than raw tooling
- Internal composability can connect web apps, shared data stores, and design authoring environments
- Curation, discoverability, and feedback loops are necessary so platform tools stay used over time
- Standardized platform services reduce duplicate implementation across teams
- Community contribution and integration patterns are key for scaling platform ecosystems
