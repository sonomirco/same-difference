The platformization of AEC is reshaping power, control, and data flows in the built environment. Autodesk's dominance through Revit and cloud infrastructure represents a fundamental shift in how construction data is created, managed, and monetized.

## BIM shifts: From 2D to data

BIM shifts design and construction from 2D plans and speculative renderings to a **spreadsheet aesthetic** based on:
- Construction components
- Data
- Validating design decisions on numerical ground
- Boardroom discussions around economic policy and project viability

## BIM diffusion and standardization

### Government-driven adoption

The diffusion of BIM has been strongly driven by governments for its promises to:
- Reduce risks
- Speed up project delivery
- Ensure transparency
- Foster collaboration and partnership between clients, architects, engineers, construction firms, and public authorities

### The standardization challenge

**One of the most critical impediments**: Lack of uniform standards for data and model exchange.

While demand for consistent data and model standards has existed since introducing CAD and later BIM, the standardization process has not been straightforward.

### IFC entanglement

IFC standardization is closely intertwined with technical development of BIM software:
- The standard influences technology development
- Distinguishing standardization efforts from software tool development is difficult
- It's both a technical and social process

## Cloud-API solutions and vendor lock-in

With combined cloud-API solutions, the software industry may bridge:
- Companies
- Processes
- Data

This decreases chances for actors to:
- Employ non-proprietary software applications
- Keep working in accustomed software environments

## openBIM: The resistance

### What openBIM represents

**openBIM** = IFC file sharing format for interoperability between different BIM software and data

A viable alternative for:
- SMEs
- Those with different software preferences beyond proprietary systems

### Alternative approaches

1. **Architectural firms warning Autodesk** - Unionize to regain control over tools
2. **Open-source BIM applications**:
   - BlenderBIM (add-on to community-developed Blender)
   - Speckle (open-source BIM data platform)
   - Open Design Alliance (software development kits)
   - ifcOpenShell (open-source software libraries)

### The reality

These remain feasible options for only a small group of initiates in academia and AEC experts.

## Autodesk's platform dominance

### The meta-platform

Autodesk drives the platformization of architecture and construction:
- Defining large parts of the sector's work and philosophy
- Enabled by intertwining of governmental policy with techno-economic platform logic:
  - Subscription models
  - Open APIs
  - Cloud-enabled collaboration for better data and software interoperability

### Core customer shift

Autodesk is shifting toward:
- Engineers as core customers
- Construction companies as core customers
- Architects depend on them to build their designs

### The consequence

BIM becoming an obligatory passage point gives tremendous power to individual software companies to define:
- What construction data should look like
- Which data is collected and which is not
- How data can be used and reused
- What is ultimately being built based on software tools in use

## Power and control in the platform era

### Where power resides

Power and control are no longer in the relation between designers and their tools. They've moved into:
- The structure of proprietary design software
- The operations of (cloud) platforms

### Cross-subsidization

Software providers empower platformization strategy by:
- Cross-subsidizing cloud platforms with revenue from software licenses
- Emerging market for cloud solutions will exceed software distribution in the long run

### The platform logic

It would be naïve to assess digital transformation risks and benefits without considering:
- The techno-economic logic
- The favorable political landscape

Autodesk has:
- Turned Revit into prime BIM-based data source
- Locked in most BIM users worldwide
- Is becoming a "meta-platform" through cloud infrastructure

## Summary

The platformization of AEC represents a fundamental power shift:

1. **BIM as data** - From 2D plans to component-based, data-driven decisions
2. **Standardization struggles** - Lack of uniform data and model exchange standards
3. **Cloud-API lock-in** - Decreasing ability to use non-proprietary software
4. **openBIM resistance** - Open-source alternatives remain niche
5. **Autodesk dominance** - Meta-platform through Revit and cloud infrastructure
6. **Power shift** - Control moved from designers to platform owners
7. **Cross-subsidization** - Software licenses fund cloud platform expansion

The question for AEC firms: Are you building on someone else's platform, or building your own platform?
