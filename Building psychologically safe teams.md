Building psychologically safe teams requires intentional design of team structures, ownership models, and inclusive cultures. There's no "ideal" org structure—each comes with tradeoffs that teams must navigate daily.

## Team structures: Tradeoffs and realities

I've operated in centralized, decentralized, federated, and hybrid models. They all have bugs and features.

### Centralized
**Pros**: Greater governance, less technical debt
**Cons**: Less nimble, not as much subject matter expertise across brands/domains/BUs

### Decentralized
**Pros**: Businesses have complete control and autonomy (which they love!)
**Cons**: A "cluster f*" all around—duplication, inconsistency, lack of standards

### Federated/Hybrid
**Pros**: Can work well
**Cons**: Requires tight, integrated, and aligned processes—most enterprises lack this kind of maturity

### Hub & Spoke
**Pros**: Centralized governance with pods in the business; business sets priorities while governance, best practices, tools managed at center
**Cons**: Only works when you're "BFFs" with IT and they know the domain well

### The temporal component

When starting, begin with a **centralized team** and work on:
- Building standards
- Best practices
- Tech stack

As you show success and grow, think about the **hub and spoke model**.

**Key insight**: There is no "ideal" org structure—each comes with a bag of sand you'll have to swallow each day.

## Ownership: A powerful motivating force

Ownership is one of the most motivating forces there is.

### What creates ownership

From [Why Ownership is a Powerful Motivating Force](https://medium.com/swlh/why-ownership-is-one-of-the-most-motivating-forces-there-is-651fc6c205ca):

This doesn't require large-scale reimagining of job descriptions:

- Some businesses let employees create their own titles
- Costs nothing but has powerful effect on sense of ownership
- Cross-functional teams given flexibility and independence
- Freedom fosters creativity and produces best work
- People work on projects they can be proud of

> "They're contributing to the company's overall goals, yes, but they are also working on projects they can be proud of."

## Feature factory: 12 signs you're working in one

From [12 Signs You're Working in a Feature Factory](https://cutle.fish/blog/12-signs-youre-working-in-a-feature-factory):

Feature factories focus on output volume rather than outcome value. Signs include:

1. Measuring success by features shipped
2. No clear connection between work and business outcomes
3. Teams are treated as delivery resources
4. Product managers are project managers with a different title
5. No time for discovery or research
6. Decisions made by HiPPO (Highest Paid Person's Opinion)
7. Teams aren't empowered to say no
8. Success is "on time, on budget" not "solved the problem"
9. No learning or iteration after launch
10. Teams constantly shuffled between projects
11. No clear product vision or strategy
12. Innovation is discouraged in favor of "safe" features

**Alternative**: Focus on outcomes, empower teams, invest in discovery, and measure by customer value delivered.

## Team Topologies: Breaking down silos

Team Topologies can initiate discussions about:
- Breaking down silos
- Establishing value streams

However, [some argue](https://martyoo.medium.com/stop-team-topologies-fd954ea26eca) it's not a silver bullet—context matters.

**Useful aspects**:
- Thinking in terms of value streams
- Understanding team interaction modes
- Designing teams around business domains

**Caveats**:
- Don't apply dogmatically without understanding context
- Focus on outcomes, not just team structures

## Founder mode vs manager mode

Paul Graham's [Founder Mode](https://paulgraham.com/foundermode.html) explores how founders should lead differently from professional managers.

### Traditional management advice

- Delegate and avoid micromanagement
- Hire good people and let them do their jobs
- Scale through hierarchy and process

### Why this harms startups

Traditional management assumes the goal is to run the company like a large corporation. But founders have unique instincts and strengths vital to success.

### Founder mode

- Founders stay deeply involved in operations even as company scales
- Direct relationships throughout the organization
- Skip levels when needed
- Trust founder instincts over conventional management wisdom

> "Founder mode may prove more complex but ultimately more effective than traditional management approaches."

**Reference**: [Overlap Tech on Founder Mode](https://x.com/overlap_tech/status/1830321952218841460)

## Collective genius: Everyone at the table

From [The Rise of Software as a Workforce](https://www.unknownarts.co/p/the-rise-of-software-as-a-workforce):

> "If one function has a monopoly on creativity and we disregard everybody else's voice, we just gave room for our competitors to take up market share. It's the people who don't have institutional knowledge who sometimes could be the most innovative change makers because they're not going to know what's conventional and accepted. They're going to come from a place of pure interest."

### Creating inclusive creativity

- Engender curiosity as the norm
- Ensure all important functions sit at the table
- Include them in creative discussions so they can dream with us
- Value diverse perspectives over institutional knowledge

### Sports team analogy

> "To win a championship, everybody is important. Whether you're on the field, off the field, pre-game, post-game—you're an incredible part of the vision and purpose."

**Reference**: [Building One with Tomer Cohen: Jason Mayden on Jordan Brand](https://podcasts.apple.com/au/podcast/building-one-with-tomer-cohen/id1726672498?i=1000683902794)

## Summary

Building psychologically safe teams requires:

1. **Right org structure for your context** - No ideal model, understand tradeoffs
2. **Ownership and autonomy** - Let teams own their work and take pride in it
3. **Avoid feature factories** - Focus on outcomes, not output
4. **Value all perspectives** - Include everyone in creative discussions
5. **Founder mode when appropriate** - Deep involvement can beat delegation
6. **Psychological safety** - Create environments where people can speak up, experiment, and learn

The goal: Teams where people feel ownership, can do their best work, and contribute to collective genius.
