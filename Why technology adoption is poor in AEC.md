Technology adoption in AEC is notoriously poor. The reasons are structural: misalignment between tools and business success metrics, crossing the adoption chasm twice, centralized vs decentralized tensions, and software that doesn't understand customer needs.

## Stakeholder alignment and misalignment

From [Proving Ground: Three Ideas for Digital Strategies](https://provingground.io/2025/01/22/three-ideas-for-developing-digital-strategies-to-support-smart-healthy-and-fit-businesses/):

### The misalignment problem

> "Misalignment between a specific tactic and how business success is measured can create a significant disconnect for communicating why the tactic is being adopted in the first place."

### The solution

> "All stakeholders – including and especially business leaders – need to be brought on board and be provided guidance in how they should participate."

## Crossing the adoption chasm twice

From [Place r Solutions: Crossing the Chasm Twice](https://placersolutions.medium.com/crossing-the-chasm-twice-construction-techs-enduring-challenge-fea7bf9d0b55):

> "The harsh reality construction tech startups face is the need to cross the adoption chasm not once, but twice."

### The two chasms

1. **Early adopters to early majority** - The classic Geoffrey Moore chasm
2. **Early majority to late majority** - The construction industry's lagging adoption

## Centralization vs decentralization in AEC

From AIA report observations:

> "From the AIA report, we see an increasing centralization, while on the other side, developing prospecting, we see a more decentralized teams."

### If projects are products...

If we consider projects as products and the cross-functional teams that work on them, why is the tendency in AEC to centralize?

### The missing piece

> "In AEC, we keep working in silence, there is no cross-communication from the team, and there is no common infrastructure used to deliver projects and reuse solutions."

## Smoothing out tech adoption

From [Todd Weyandt: Smoothing Out Tech Adoption](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/smoothing-out-tech-adoption-todd-weyandt-mqgzc/):

Technology adoption requires smoothing the path—reducing friction, addressing concerns, and making new approaches feel safe rather than threatening.

## SME contractors and spreadsheet usage

From [SME Contractors and Their Spreadsheet Usage](https://media.licdn.com/dms/document/media/v2/D561FAQEHOLbVgVrjhQ/feedshare-document-pdf-analyzed/feedshare-document-pdf-analyzed/0/1691565719812):

### The critical divide

Two groups emerge among SME contractors:

1. **Active spreadsheet customizers** - Fill gaps left by commercial software
2. **Those lacking awareness** - Of critical operational methods or lacking capability to implement structured digital means

### The problem with software

> "Software developers appear to have not pursued the clear Lean ideal of 'perfection'—all their client's needs and wants have not been met."

Technology providers aim for advanced solutions, but fundamental business needs remain underserved.

### Why spreadsheets are default

**1. Flexibility and familiarity**
- Unparalleled flexibility
- Quick formula creation and adjustment
- No forced workflows
- Employees comfortable after decades of use

**2. Cost-effectiveness**
- High acquisition cost of commercial software deters adoption
- Ongoing investment and mastery time
- Spreadsheets widely available, often part of standard office packages
- No additional licensing fees or vendor training

**3. Customization and adaptability**
- SME contractors need: dual overhead rates, job sizing adjustments, resource forecasting
- Commercial software doesn't support these practices universally
- Contractors create spreadsheet models for specific needs
- "Not creating well-established practices in industry-standard software will contribute to continuing malaise"

## Misaligned customer needs

### Lack of field research

- Many construction software solutions designed with large firms in mind
- Large firms have IT specialists and dedicated software administrators
- SME contractors lack these resources and need intuitive, ready-to-use tools

### Delayed feedback loops

- Unlike industries with rapid iteration based on real-time feedback
- Construction software undergoes long development cycles
- Changes implemented infrequently

### Limited awareness of on-site needs

- Software developers focus on digital transformation trends
- Not understanding practical, on-the-ground challenges
- SMEs don't receive tailored solutions addressing actual workflows

### The solution: Process-centric software development

> "Instead of attempting to replace spreadsheets outright, software developers should integrate more closely with SME contractors' workflows to capture and enhance their existing processes."

> "Tailoring construction software to enable effective practices while 'hardwiring' them into a company's process could lessen industry problems."

### Required shifts

1. **Co-creation with contractors** - Engage during software development to understand pain points
2. **Modular and customizable software** - Configure features based on specific needs, not one-size-fits-all
3. **Accessible feedback mechanisms** - Continuous loops where contractors report inefficiencies and request features

## Customer feedback problems

From Holmstrom et al. (2012):

> "Today, software development is conducted in a chaotic environment... disordered and dynamic markets, complicated and uncertain customer demands, shorter development cycle time pressures, and Moore's Law effects."

### The fundamental issue

> "In many software development firms, product managers struggle to get well-defined customer perceptions, needs and wants."

> "Software value and utility validation occurs after deployment. Furthermore, learning from customers is neither typically formalized nor continuous."

## Summary

Technology adoption in AEC is poor because:

1. **Stakeholder misalignment** - Tactics don't connect to business success metrics
2. **Crossing chasm twice** - Construction tech faces adoption hurdles twice
3. **Centralization paradox** - Centralizing while needing decentralization for agility
4. **Working in silence** - No cross-communication, no common infrastructure
5. **Spreadsheets fill gaps** - Commercial software doesn't meet SME needs
6. **Misaligned customer needs** - Software designed for large firms, not SME realities
7. **Delayed feedback loops** - Long development cycles without continuous learning
8. **Validation after deployment** - Learning from customers not formalized or continuous

The path forward: Co-creation with users, modular software, accessible feedback, and process-centric development that enhances existing workflows rather than replacing them.
