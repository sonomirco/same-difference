New business models are emerging at the intersection of software and hardware, open source, and vertical integration. At the same time, traditional models like consulting are facing their breaking point.

## Vertical integration: Software + hardware

From [Vertical Integrators (Not Boring)](https://www.notboring.co/p/vertical-integrators):

The relationship between software and hardware is creating new business model opportunities through vertical integration—companies that own both the technology and the physical delivery.

## Business and IT alignment: The new operating model

> "When business teams work hand in hand with IT, they can address fluid requirements effectively. This new operating model—where business and IT align more closely—positions companies in the AEC space for digital success."

### The trust barrier

People fear being replaced by tools. Engineers with years of experience resist change because their techniques have worked in the past.

There's logic for them saying "let's do what has always worked." This mindset must be overcome by:
1. **Building trust first** - Once people trust the team and service, they're willing to try different approaches
2. **Demonstrating value** - Show, don't just tell
3. **Service over tools** - It's about how we communicate and the service we provide, not the tools

### Crossing the Chasm

Using Geoffrey Moore's framework:

- **CompDesign** are early adopters of processes and technologies
- They're "pre-chasm" thinkers—only about 15% of the curve
- They get excited about new technical solutions but struggle to convince colleagues
- **The challenge**: Communicating in ways that reach the early majority

## Open source in AEC

### Overcoming misconceptions

Open source is often misunderstood in AEC—as if it means giving away valuable IP for free.

### The opportunity

Companies could push innovation by making APIs available as open-source projects:
- Positions companies as leaders in digital innovation
- Attracts top talent
- Builds culture of collaboration and transparency
- Taps into broader pool of expertise
- Accelerates innovation cycles

### From Guy Martin (Autodesk, NVIDIA)

> "Open source and inner-source approaches enable internal and external collaboration, promote culture where teams can address common industry challenges."

### Amazon's API mandate

Jeff Bezos's API mandate was not just technical—it was strategic vision:

- Teams learned not to rely on each other directly
- Build interfaces that could be consumed by others (internally and externally)
- Fostered agility and responsiveness
- Enabled quick adaptation to market changes
- Created environment conducive to innovation, collaboration, and scalability

**Reference**: [Open Source and Inner Source at Autodesk (OSP0)](https://ospo.co/case-studies/open-source-and-innersource-at-autodesk/)

## The consulting industry breaking point

From [LinkedIn: The Consulting Industry is Reaching a Breaking Point](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/usmans_the-consulting-industry-is-reaching-a-breaking-activity-7293191747147325441-BLm6/):

### Not from AI, not from clients—but from within

The evidence:
- McKinsey's premium fees down 15% in key segments
- EY's failed $700M transformation attempt
- Deloitte's partner profits shrinking while costs rise
- Big 4 transaction revenue falling double digits

### What holds them back

- Every major change needs majority consensus
- Partners choose quick profits over investment
- Innovation dies by committee
- Culture resists reinvention

### Sound familiar?

In 1920, factory owners faced the same paralysis with electricity:
- Bolted motors onto old machines
- Expected magical productivity gains
- Blamed the technology when it failed

### Making the same mistake today

Today's consulting giants:
- Adding AI to broken processes
- Protecting legacy profit pools
- Resisting structural change
- Mistaking size for safety

### The market isn't waiting

- Clients build internal capabilities faster than ever
- Premium fees no longer justify the value
- AI collapses the traditional pyramid
- Specialized firms take high-value work

## Summary

New business models require:

1. **Vertical integration** - Combining software and hardware for competitive advantage
2. **Business/IT alignment** - New operating model for digital success
3. **Trust over tools** - Build relationships before introducing change
4. **Cross the chasm** - Communicate to early majority, not just early adopters
5. **Embrace open source** - Share APIs, collaborate, build ecosystems
6. **Learn from Amazon** - API mandate as strategic vision, not just technical
7. **Avoid consultant trap** - Don't bolt AI onto broken processes

The consulting industry's crisis offers a warning: Protecting legacy models while the market changes around you is a recipe for irrelevance.
