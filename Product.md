This note consolidates product strategy material across market fit, product-led growth patterns, feature team dynamics, productization in AEC, and outcome-based business models. The combined view emphasizes that successful AEC product development starts with customer and workflow understanding, then iterates toward scalable product and operating models.

Across these sources, the shared pattern is consistent: validate demand early, define measurable value, avoid building in isolation, and connect product decisions to business outcomes and adoption constraints in real delivery contexts.

Referenced sources include product-market fit frameworks [R1], Miro growth patterns [R3], feature team vs feature factory perspectives [R6][R7], and AEC productization resources [R8][R14].

## Key points

- Product market fit requires explicit hypotheses, customer segmentation, and outcome-based success metrics
- Product discovery and curation should happen before heavy solution investment
- Product-led growth patterns benefit from multiple entry points and strong collaborative workflows
- Feature teams create value when aligned to business outcomes rather than output volume
- In AEC, productization often starts from services and transitions toward standardized, reusable delivery models
- Outcome-as-a-service models can reduce adoption friction when customers buy results, not only software access
- Modularization and composability improve long-term scalability of product ecosystems
