The shared pattern across product strategy, product-led growth, and AEC productisation is consistent: validate demand early, define measurable value, avoid building in isolation, and connect product decisions to business outcomes and adoption constraints in real delivery contexts.

Product market fit requires explicit hypotheses, customer segmentation, and outcome-based success metrics — not shipping and hoping. Product discovery and curation should happen before heavy solution investment. Product-led growth benefits from multiple entry points and strong collaborative workflows; Miro's growth path is a useful reference for how a collaborative tool scales by making collaboration itself more valuable.

Feature teams create value when aligned to business outcomes rather than output volume. The distinction between a feature team and a feature factory is whether the team is measuring what changed for users, not just what shipped.

**In AEC, productisation most often starts from services and transitions toward standardised, reusable delivery models.** Outcome-as-a-service models can reduce adoption friction when customers buy results rather than software access. Modularisation and composability improve long-term scalability of product ecosystems — the internet's modular logic applies as readily to engineering workflows as to software architecture.
