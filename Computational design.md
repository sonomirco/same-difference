Computational design should be treated as a capability system, not a set of isolated automation tasks. The goal is to move from reactive delivery support to a model that continuously improves how teams design, coordinate, and make decisions.

The operating model that works is CDaaS — Computational Design as a Service — where a dedicated team provides computational design capability to delivery projects as a service, while systematically turning repeated work into reusable assets. It has four explicit responsibilities: service delivery (support projects with automation and decision support), workflow redesign (identify process bottlenecks and improve end-to-end flow), productisation (convert repeated solutions into stable templates and internal tools), and enablement (train teams, document standards, increase adoption). CDaaS solves immediate delivery problems and builds long-term capability at the same time.

**The three-layer structure that makes this compound over time is: service layer first, then product layer, then knowledge layer.** The service layer supports live projects and validates real-world needs quickly. The product layer converts proven service patterns into scalable internal tools and reusable components. The knowledge layer documents decisions and standards so each project improves the next one. The rule that holds the whole model together: every successful pilot should leave reusable infrastructure behind, not just a one-off fix.

Scaling follows a specific path: start with a small core team that delivers directly on projects. Once methods are stable, replicate through trained local teams using shared standards, playbooks, and tooling. The core team then shifts toward enablement, governance, and platform support. Asset-light scaling with centralized quality and learning.

The traps to avoid: building features without clear outcome hypotheses, treating computational teams as permanent firefighters, optimising only for efficiency while ignoring adoption, and adding disconnected tools instead of strengthening the ecosystem backbone.
